Hydrogen is the lightest element in the universe. Storing enough of it to power a car is one of the harder engineering problems in clean mobility.

Hydrogen gas is compressed to **700 bar** — roughly 10,000 psi. At that pressure, 7.5 kg fits into about 126 litres — enough for over 600 km.

**Type III** — aluminium liner wrapped in carbon fibre. Better for 350 bar and larger vehicles.

**Type IV** — high-density polymer liner wrapped in carbon fibre. Up to 70% lighter. This is the standard for passenger FCEVs like the Toyota Mirai and Hyundai Nexo.

Safety testing includes:
- Burst pressure tested to over 1,575 bar
- Drop tested from 1.8 metres
- Fire tested
- Pressure relief devices fitted

The engineering challenge is not whether it can be done safely. It already is — in vehicles on the road today.
