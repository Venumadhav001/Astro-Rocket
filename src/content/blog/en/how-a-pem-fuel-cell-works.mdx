Most people have heard of hydrogen fuel cells. Very few know what actually happens inside one.

A PEM fuel cell has three core parts: an anode, a membrane, and a cathode.

**At the anode:** Hydrogen enters and splits into protons and electrons.

**Through the membrane:** The protons pass through. The electrons cannot — they are forced through an external circuit. That external circuit is your electricity.

**At the cathode:** Everything reunites with oxygen from the air. The only output is water vapour.

No combustion. No CO₂. Just hydrogen in, electricity and water out.

Efficiency sits around 50 to 60% — significantly higher than a combustion engine at 20 to 35%.

That is what makes it interesting. The physics are fundamentally cleaner.
