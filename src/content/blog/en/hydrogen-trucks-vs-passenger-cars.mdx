Not all hydrogen vehicles are the same conversation.

**Long-haul trucks** operate 10–20 hours a day, 500+ km routes. A battery large enough for a 30-tonne truck can weigh 2–4 tonnes — eating into payload. Hydrogen refuels in minutes like diesel. That matters enormously for commercial operators.

**Passenger cars** average 40–60 km daily. BEVs handle that comfortably. The argument for hydrogen in passenger cars is weaker today — not because the technology fails, but because infrastructure is not there yet.

The commercial side is already moving. Hyundai's Xcient fuel cell truck has been operating commercially in Switzerland since 2020. Daimler Truck, Volvo, and MAN all have active programmes.

Hydrogen does not need to win everywhere to matter. It needs to win where batteries genuinely cannot. Heavy transport is that place.
