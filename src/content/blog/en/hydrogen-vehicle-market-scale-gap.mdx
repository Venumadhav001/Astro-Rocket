There are fewer than **100,000 hydrogen vehicles** on roads globally. In 2023, over **10 million BEVs** were sold. The gap is real.

Getting to 1% of global vehicle sales requires five things at the same time:

**1. Green hydrogen below USD 2/kg** — currently USD 4–9/kg. Requires electrolyser manufacturing to scale significantly.

**2. Refuelling infrastructure ×10** — ~1,000 stations today concentrated in Japan, South Korea, Germany, and California.

**3. More manufacturers committed to volume** — today Toyota Mirai and Hyundai Nexo carry almost the entire passenger segment.

**4. Consistent long-term policy** — incentives that change every election cycle cannot anchor infrastructure investment.

**5. Actually green hydrogen** — scaling on grey hydrogen undermines the entire climate case.

All five are interdependent. Each one is waiting for the others to move first. That is why progress is slower than the engineering alone would suggest.
