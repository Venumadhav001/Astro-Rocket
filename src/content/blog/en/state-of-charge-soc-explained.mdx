**SOC — State of Charge** is the battery equivalent of a fuel gauge. 0 to 100%.

In a fuel cell hybrid vehicle it becomes a critical control parameter.

- **SOC below 20%** → Fuel cell increases output to recharge battery
- **SOC between 20–80%** → Normal operation, load split between both
- **SOC above 80%** → Regenerative braking limited, nowhere to store energy

Every energy management decision starts with reading the SOC.

It also affects battery lifetime. Lithium-ion batteries degrade faster when charged to 100% or discharged to 0% repeatedly. Keeping SOC between 20 and 80% significantly extends service life.

SOC management is not just monitoring. It is an active control input that shapes how the entire powertrain behaves.
