Most people focus on the fuel cell. But there is another component just as important — the motor.

In FCEVs, that motor is almost always a **PMSM — Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motor**.

The drivetrain chain:
1. Fuel cell generates **DC electricity**
2. DC-DC converter **regulates voltage**
3. Inverter **converts DC to three-phase AC**
4. PMSM **converts AC to mechanical torque at the wheels**

No combustion. No gearbox. No exhaust.

**Why PMSM?**
- **Efficiency:** 92 to 97% — better than induction motors at 85 to 92%
- **Torque:** Full torque from zero speed, no gearbox needed
- **Durability:** Brushless design, less wear
- **Regen braking:** Acts as generator during deceleration

The Toyota Mirai uses a PMSM. The Hyundai Nexo uses a PMSM. It is the industry standard for a reason.
