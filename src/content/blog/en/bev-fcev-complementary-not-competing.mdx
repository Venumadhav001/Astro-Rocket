BEV vs FCEV. Everyone picks a side. The honest answer is that comparing them directly misses the point.

**BEVs** convert 70 to 90% of stored electricity into motion. Hard to beat on efficiency. Great for urban driving and short to medium distances.

**FCEVs** operate at 25 to 35% well-to-wheel efficiency due to the energy cost of producing and compressing hydrogen. But they refuel in 3 to 5 minutes, offer 500 to 650 km range, and suit long-haul and high-payload applications where the physics favour hydrogen.

BEV and FCEV are not competing technologies. They are complementary solutions for different parts of the mobility system.

The future of zero-emission transport likely needs both.
