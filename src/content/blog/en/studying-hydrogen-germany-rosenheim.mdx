I moved from Hyderabad to Rosenheim, Bavaria, to do an M.Sc. in Hydrogen Technology. I thought the biggest challenge would be the technical content. It was not.

**The academic environment is genuinely rigorous.** At TH Rosenheim you are expected to find the answer, form a view, and defend it. That was a bigger adjustment than I expected.

**Precision is not optional.** Vague does not pass. That habit has changed how I approach everything.

**The language barrier is real.** My German is at A2 level. Rosenheim is not an international city. You either adapt or you stay stuck. I am still adapting.

**Industry is closer than I expected.** TH Rosenheim sits between Munich and Salzburg. The companies I want to work for are not far away.

**The research is applied.** My thesis is on a real system — not a hypothetical.

The degree is important. But the bigger education has been learning how to operate in an environment where you start at zero and figure it out.

*Kalluri Devi Narasimha Venu Madhav — M.Sc. Hydrogen Technology, TH Rosenheim*
