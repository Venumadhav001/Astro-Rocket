Hydrogen has been called the fuel of the future for decades. So why is it still not mainstream?

Three reasons.

**First: Infrastructure.** There are fewer than 1,000 hydrogen filling stations worldwide. For comparison, there are over 170,000 public EV charging points in Europe alone. No stations means no adoption.

**Second: Cost.** Green hydrogen currently costs around 4 to 6 euros per kilogram to produce. For it to compete commercially, that number needs to come down to around 1 to 2 euros. Electrolyser costs are dropping — but not fast enough yet.

**Third: Awareness.** Most people cannot explain the difference between a battery EV and a fuel cell vehicle. Low awareness means low demand, which slows down investment.

Infrastructure is growing slowly across Europe and Japan. Production costs are falling year on year. These are not unsolvable problems.

The technology is ready. The ecosystem is not yet.
