Two hydrogen cars. Two completely different approaches.

The **Toyota Mirai** is a sedan. The **Hyundai Nexo** is an SUV. Same fuel — very different vehicles.

| Spec | Toyota Mirai | Hyundai Nexo |
|---|---|---|
| Power | 182 hp | 161 hp |
| Torque | 300 Nm (RWD) | 395 Nm (FWD) |
| Range | ~647 km | ~611 km |
| Refuel time | 3–5 min | 3–5 min |
| CO₂ emissions | Zero | Zero |

The Mirai is positioned as a premium sedan competing with vehicles like the BMW 5 Series. The Nexo is a practical family SUV.

Both produce zero CO₂ emissions. The only exhaust output is water vapour.

These are the **only two hydrogen fuel cell passenger vehicles currently in mass production worldwide.** Infrastructure remains the biggest limiting factor for both. Range and refuel time are not the problem.
