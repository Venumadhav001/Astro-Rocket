When testing how efficient a vehicle is, you need a standard driving pattern that everyone uses. That standard is the **WLTP — Worldwide Harmonised Light Vehicle Test Procedure**.

It covers four phases:

- **Low speed** — city traffic
- **Medium speed** — suburban roads
- **High speed** — rural roads
- **Extra high speed** — motorway

Combined, the cycle runs for about 30 minutes and covers roughly 23 km.

What makes it more realistic than older standards is the mix. Earlier standards like the **NEDC** used very simple, slow driving patterns that gave efficiency numbers far from real-world conditions. WLTP changed that.

For hydrogen vehicles, this matters more than most. Fuel cell efficiency changes depending on load. The WLTP captures the full range.

That is why it is the standard evaluation cycle used in fuel cell vehicle research today.
