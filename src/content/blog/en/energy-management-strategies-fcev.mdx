When a fuel cell and a battery work together in a vehicle, someone has to decide who does what. That decision is called an **energy management strategy** — and it matters more than most people think.

**1. Rule-Based Control**
Simple predefined rules decide when the fuel cell takes over and when the battery steps in. The fuel cell handles the base load; the battery covers the peaks. Easy to implement, computationally light, and reliable.

**2. Load-Following Control**
The fuel cell follows the power demand in real time. The battery keeps the State of Charge stable. More adaptive but the fuel cell experiences more frequent load changes — which over time can affect stack durability.

**3. Efficiency-Oriented Control**
The fuel cell is kept near its optimal efficiency point at all times. The battery absorbs all the fluctuations. This uses the least hydrogen but requires more complex control logic.

No single strategy is best in every situation. The right choice depends on the drive cycle, the vehicle class, and what you are optimising for: range, durability, or simplicity.
