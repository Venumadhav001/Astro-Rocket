Comparing vehicles only at the tailpipe is like judging a restaurant by the last five minutes of cooking.

**Well-to-Wheel (WtW)** covers the full energy chain:
- **Well-to-Tank:** Production, processing, delivery of fuel
- **Tank-to-Wheel:** Conversion of stored energy into motion
- **Well-to-Wheel:** The full chain — the only fair comparison

**WtW efficiency:**
- BEVs on clean grid: ~65–77%
- FCEVs on green hydrogen: ~25–35%

But a BEV on a coal-heavy grid looks very different on emissions. An FCEV on green hydrogen looks much better even with lower efficiency. The energy source changes the conclusion more than the ratio does.

Neither powertrain dominates on every metric. It depends on your grid, your hydrogen source, and what you are optimising for.
