In 2020 the EU set some of the most ambitious hydrogen targets in the world. By 2030, the plan was to produce 10 million tonnes of green hydrogen domestically, install 40 GW of electrolyser capacity, and build 11,600 km of hydrogen pipelines.

Four years later, the gap is significant.

Current green hydrogen production sits at around **0.02 million tonnes per year**. Of the 7.2 million tonnes consumed in Europe today, **99.7% still comes from fossil fuels**.

The challenges are real — green hydrogen is 3 to 4 times more expensive than grey, subsidy approvals take 12 to 24 months, and China leads global electrolyser manufacturing.

None of this means the strategy is wrong. It means execution has not caught up with ambition yet.

The direction is right. The timeline was always going to be difficult.
