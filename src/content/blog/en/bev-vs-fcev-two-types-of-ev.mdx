Most people think there is only one type of electric vehicle. There are actually two — and they work very differently.

**The first is a Battery EV.** Plug in, charge, drive. Simple.

**The second is a Fuel Cell EV.** Fill it with hydrogen in 3 to 5 minutes. The fuel cell converts it into electricity on the go. The only emission is water vapour.

My thesis looks at combining both.

A Fuel Cell–Battery Hybrid Vehicle uses a PEM fuel cell as the main power source, with a lithium-ion battery handling the peaks — sudden acceleration, regenerative braking, demand spikes.

The challenge is not building the system. It is managing both together intelligently.

- When does the fuel cell take over?
- When does the battery step in?
- How do you keep hydrogen consumption low while keeping the battery healthy?

That is what I am currently working on — simulating this in MATLAB over the WLTP driving cycle.
