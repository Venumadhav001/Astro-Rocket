Everyone in the hydrogen space talks about colours. But what do they actually mean?

**Grey Hydrogen** — 95% of global production. Natural gas via Steam Methane Reforming, no carbon capture. ~10 kg CO₂ per kg of hydrogen.

**Blue Hydrogen** — Same SMR process but with Carbon Capture and Storage. Emissions drop 85–95%. Costs USD 1.5–3/kg today.

**Green Hydrogen** — Renewable electricity splits water through electrolysis. Near-zero emissions. Currently USD 4–9/kg but falling fast.

Blue hydrogen makes sense as a transitional option — gas infrastructure already exists. But methane leakage from upstream supply chains is a concern that rarely gets enough attention.

The industry direction is clearly green. The debate is about timeline and investment priority — not destination.
